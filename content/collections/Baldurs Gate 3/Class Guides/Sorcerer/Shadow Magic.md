## Build Snapshot

### Fantasy / Flavor
- Deal in the sepulchral darkness and shadow arts.
- Blend melee attacks and spellcasting into one build.
- Use magical darkness to hit enemies while they struggle to hit you.

### Core Playstyle
- Play up close with a summoned psychic-damage weapon while leveraging magical darkness and Sorcerer Metamagic, then invest into Paladin to spend spell slots on Divine Smite.

### Party Role
- Frontline melee burst damage (with spell slot-fueled strikes).
- Ranged AOE burst damage option (via Sorcerer spellcasting).
- Utility/defense through invisibility, reactions, and positioning tools.

### Pros
- Melee and Range
- Heavy Armour Spellcaster
- Psychic Damage Burst

### Cons
- Slow Start
- Multiclass
- Complex Spells

### Power Spikes
- Level 1: Early power from armour/weapon proficiencies (Paladin start).
- Level 4: Shadow Blade becomes available and becomes a main damage source.
- Level 6: Access to Level 3 spells (Fireball) and defensive Counterspell swap.
- Level 8: Paladin 2 for Fighting Style + Divine Smite.
- Level 11: Paladin 5 for Extra Attack (plus key Oath features listed).

## Character Creation
- Race: Half-Orc
- Subrace: (Not specified in source)
- Class: Paladin (first level only), then multiclass into Sorcerer
- Subclass: Oath of Vengeance (Paladin), Shadow Magic (Sorcerer)
- Background: Entertainer
- Ability Scores: STR 8, DEX 16, CON 14, INT 8, WIS 10, CHA 17
- Proficiencies: Athletics, Persuasion; Armour Proficiency: Light Armour, Medium Armour, Heavy Armour, and Shield; Weapon Proficiency: Simple Weapons and Martial Weapons; Saving Throw: Strength and Charisma

## Level Progression

### Level 1
- Gain:
  - Paladin (start for armour and weapon proficiencies “that aren’t innate to the Sorcerer class”)
  - Subclass: Oath of Vengeance
  - Channeled Oath Charges
  - Lay on Hands
  - Divine Sense
  - Inquisitor’s Might
- Play Style:
  - Notes:
    - Start with 1 level in Paladin up front “because you want to get the Armor and Weapon Proficiencies.”
    - You can alternatively dip two levels in Paladin to gain Divine Smite, but “1 level in Paladin to gain access to proficiencies is optimal.”

### Level 2
- Gain:
  - Multiclass into Sorcerer
  - Subclass: Shadow Magic
  - Eyes of the Dark (see in the dark up to 24 meters)
  - Strength of the Grave (after reaching 0 Hit Points, regain 1 instead of becoming downed)
  - Cantrips: Booming Blade, Shocking Grasp, Ray of Frost, Fire Bolt
  - Spells: Shield, Thunderwave
- Play Style:
  - Notes:
    - “At this stage, you play in melee range with Booming Blade in heavy armour with one hand and shield.”
    - Shadow Blade uses level 2 spell slots and will become the main source of damage later.

### Level 3
- Gain:
  - Sorcery Points
  - Metamagic: Careful Spell and Twinned Spell
  - Spell: Chromatic Orb
- Play Style:
  - Notes:
    - “At this level use Twinned for double Chromatic Orb shots.”
    - “Use Careful for the darkness duration increase.”
    - Level 3 spell loadout listed: Shield, Thunderwave, Chromatic Orb.

### Level 4
- Gain:
  - Metamagic: Quickened Spell
  - Spell: Shadow Blade
  - Replace Spell: Replace Thunderwave with Invisibility
  - Subclass spells/actions (listed here as becoming available while leveling):
    - Darkness (Cast as a level 2 spell at will.)
    - Eyes of the Dark: Darkness (Cast as a level 2 spell at will.)
- Play Style:
  - Notes:
    - “At this level, you can now hit targets with Shadow Blade, which scales off of your strength.”
    - “Spells like Mirror Image and Blur are advised if you struggle with survival (or scrolls).”
    - “You can now cast Eyes of the Darkness on top of yourself to give Shadow Blade and Advantage.”
    - “You can also attack with Booming Blade and Shadow Blade in the same action (eventually Divine Smite).”
    - Level 4 spell loadout listed: Shield, Chromatic Orb, Shadow Blade, Invisibility.

### Level 5
- Gain:
  - Feat: Ability Improvement (CHA +2)
  - Cantrip: Light
  - Spell: Mirror Image
- Play Style:
  - Notes:
    - Mirror Image: “creates three images each increasing your AC by 3.”
    - “Feel free to take Cloud of Daggers if you feel less damage at level five.”
    - “The play should mainly focus on the Chromatic Orb for area damage.”
    - Level 5 spell loadout listed: Shield, Chromatic Orb, Darkness, Eyes of the Dark: Darkness, Shadow Blade, Invisibility, Mirror Image.

### Level 6
- Gain:
  - Spells: Fireball and Counterspell
  - Replace Spell: Replace Chromatic Orb with Counterspell (noted as a recommendation)
- Play Style:
  - Notes:
    - Fireball is described as the “mega” spell; expect “massive area damage.”
    - “You are not an Evocation Wizard, so you do not want to hit friendly targets.”
    - “Placing the circle radius just at the edge to hit as many targets as possible is the key.”
    - “With Quickened Metamagic, you can cast this spell and another with a bonus action (3 Sorcery Points).”
    - “The key to mastering the damage output is using Metamagic Quickened for area-based spells and Twinned for single targets.”
    - “At this stage, you can do ranged AOE burst damage with Fireball or play in melee range with Shadow Blade and Booming Blade.”
    - Level 6 spell loadout listed: Shield, Darkness, Eyes of the Dark: Darkness, Shadow Blade, Invisibility, Mirror Image, Counterspell.

### Level 7
- Gain:
  - Spell: Misty Step
  - Shadow Magic class action: Hound of Ill Omen
- Play Style:
  - Notes:
    - Misty Step is recommended “for more mobility.”
    - “You can also use Haste if you want to hold concentration and use Twinned on you as an ally.”
    - Hound of Ill Omen: “Summons Nimbus, a shadow mastiff. Has actions Ominous Bite, Umbral Tangle, and Splinter Shadow.”

### Level 8
- Gain:
  - Switch back to Paladin (Paladin 2)
  - Fighting Style: Defence Fighting Style (+1 AC)
  - Divine Smite
  - Prepared spells to select (listed as “must select”):
    - Wrathful Smite
    - Thunderous Smite
    - Command
    - Divine Favor
    - Shield of Faith
    - Protection from Good and Evil
- Play Style:
  - Notes:
    - Divine Smite: “uses spell slot and hits in conjunction with weapon strike.”
    - You can set Divine Smite to apply automatically, only during critical hits, or via popup (reactions menu).
    - “Be cautious at first about using this too often. You will burn through your spell slots quickly at this low level.”

### Level 9
- Gain:
  - Paladin 3 (Oath of Vengeance features/spells listed)
  - Spell: Cure Wounds (recommended)
  - Oath of Vengeance unlocks listed at this level:
    - Abjure Enemy (subclass)
    - Vow of Enmity (subclass)
    - Bane (subclass)
    - Hunter’s Mark (subclass)
    - Searing Smite (selection)
- Play Style:
  - Notes:
    - This level adds “a few spells unique to the Oath of the Vengeance subclass.”

### Level 10
- Gain:
  - Paladin 4
  - Feat: Alert
- Play Style:
  - Notes:
    - “This will help you going first in the combat turn order.”
    - “For spells you can take whatever you want, Bless is helpful if not using a Cleric.”

### Level 11
- Gain:
  - Paladin 5
  - Extra Attack
  - Hold Person (Subclass Feature)
  - Misty Step (Subclass Feature)
  - Spell selections guidance: “take Aid, Lesser Restoration, and drop searing smite for Magic Weapon.”
- Play Style:
  - Notes:
    - Extra Attack: “you can attack twice, by the Attack action on your turn.”
    - “This combined with Divine Smite and the added spell slots allows you to mega single target damage pure turn.”
    - “At this level and beyond, you should feel very powerful. You can buff and debuff with a bonus action, mobility with Misty Step, and huge damage… using Smite.”

### Level 12
- Gain:
  - Paladin 6
  - Aura of Protection
  - “unlock 1 more prepared spell”
  - “able to select 1 Level 2 spell”
  - Spell note in guide: “For spells, take Protection from Poison.”
- Play Style:
  - Notes:
    - Aura of Protection: “You and nearby allies gain a bonus to Saving Throws equal to your Charisma modifier.”

## Features and Mechanics
- One of the best perks is “being able to see through the Darkness spell due to the Eyes of the Dark passive.”
- You can “cast Eyes of the Dark: Darkness on top of yourself and it will act as a barrier for ranged spells, making it near impossible for enemies to hit you.”
- You can “combo this with Shadow Blade, which increases damage when obscured,” allowing you to “excel in melee range.”
- Patch 8 introduced two new skills “that allow a spellcaster to attack in melee, scaling off of Strength: Booming Blade and Shadow Blade.”
